Organization Background:

The World Health Organization (WHO) was established on April 7, 1948,
as a specialized agency of the United Nations. It was created in response to the need for a
global organization to coordinate international efforts in public health and address
global health challenges. The WHO's constitution came into force on April 7, 1948,
making it the primary international institution responsible for promoting and
safeguarding public health on a global scale.
Past Activities of the Organization:

-Smallpox Eradication (1967-1980)
-Global Vaccination Programs
-Global Malaria Control Program
-HIV/AIDS Response
-Ebola Outbreak Response
