Involvements:

The World Health Organization (WHO) is a United Nations agency that coordinates international efforts to address global health challenges, providing guidelines, surveillance, and emergency response. It engages in research, capacity building, and partnerships to promote health equity and improve healthcare systems worldwide. It is involved in 192 Countries around the world.



Activities and Participation:

Translating science for better health emergency preparedness, Boosting climate-resilient water, sanitation and hygiene services, Ensuring monitoring and surveillance of eye care, Leading the way to improved treatments for snakebite envenoming, and Tracking SARS-CoV-2 variants.
